NCLEX Flashcards

Learn your Nursing Fundamentals

Filgrastim

Stimulate neutrophil production
Given prophylactically or if client has an infection

NSAIDS

Nephrotoxic, avoid in pt w/ kidney disease
Indomethacin, ibuprofen, naproxen
Avoid taking two NSAIDS at same time

Thiazolidinediones

Used to tx type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
Rosiflitazone, pioglitazone
Increase the risk of cardiovascular events and bladder cancer.

Scopolamine

Anticholinergic used to prevent nausea and vomiting from motion sickness.
Transdermal is placed on hairless, clean, dry area behind the ear for proper absorption.

Oxytocin

Stimulates contraction of the uterine smooth muscle. Used to induce or augment labor and prevent postpartum hemorrhage.

Acetylcysteine

Helps loosen and liquefy respiratory secretions.
Pt w/ Asthma should not receive this medication.

Atorvastatin

Prescribed to lower cholesterol and reduce the risk of atherosclerosis and coronary artery disease.
Medication should be taken in the evening or bedtime.
Myopathy is serious adverse effect
Pt w/ muscle aches should call HCP.

Methyphenidate

CNS stimulant used to tx ADHD and narcolepsy.
Common side effects include: loss of appetite, weight loss
Increased blood pressure and tachycardia

Proton Pump Inhibitors

-prazole
PPIs cause suppression of acid that would have prevented pathogens from colonizing the GI tract.
Long-term therapy may decrease absorption of calcium and promot osteoporosis.

Levetiracetam

Used in the tx of seizures.
S/E include:
Drowsiness, dizziness, unusual tiredness,
or weakness may occur.
If any of these effects last or get worse, tell your doctor or pharmacist promptly.

Lithium

Mood stabilizer used to tx bipolar disorder.
Therapeutic range is 0.6 - 1.2 mEq/L
Toxicity S/E:
diarrhea, nausea, vomiting
drowsiness, muscular weakness, dry mouth
lack of coordination

Duloxetine

SNRI, used to tx chronic pain that interferes with normal sleep patterns.

Dopamine

Inotropic med used to improve hemodynamic status in clients w/ shock and HF
Enhances cardiac output by increasing myocardial contractility.

Memantine

Used in tx for Alzheimers disease.
A N-methyl-D-aspartate antagonist, binds to NMDA receptors, blocking the brain's NMDA glutamate pathways
protecting the brain from overexposure to glutamate.

Propanolol

Nonselective beta-blocker
Decreased BP, decrease HR
Bronchoconstriction may occur due to the effect on the beta2 receptors.
Monitor pt for wheezing

Methotrexate

A folate antimetabolite, antineoplastic, immunosuppressant drug used to treat various malignancies
and as a nonbiologic disease-modifying antirheumatic drug(DMARD)
Used to tx rheumatiod arthritis and psoriasis.
Can cause bone marrow suppression, resulting in anemia, leukopenia, and thrombocytopenia.
Can increase risk for infection
Instruct pt about vaccines, avoiding crowds and infected persons.

Phenytoin

Used to tx seizures, 10-20 mcg/ml reference range
Signs of toxicity: horizontal nystagmus, gait unsteadiness.
Slurred speech, lethargy, confusion and coma.

Clozapine

Antipsychoitic medication used to mangage schizophrenia.
Severe adverse effects include: agranulocytosis, cardiac disease and seizures.
Monitor pt for s/s of infection. Sore throat, fever, flulike symptoms should be reported to HCP.

Atenolol, metoprolol

Sympathetic nervous system blockers, tx for symptoms such as
tachycardia, hypertension, irritability, tremors, and nervousness in hyperthyroidism.

Benzodiazepines

Xanax, Ativan, clonazepam, diazepam
Antianxiety drugs, should be taken at night d/t sedation effect.

Adenosine

First-line tx for paroxysmal supraventricular tachycardia (SVT: HR > 150 bpm)
Short half-life, administer rapidly over 1-2 sec., followed by 20-ml saline flush.
Monitor client for flushing, dizziness, chest pain, or palpitations during and after administration.
Repeat bolus may be needed.

Levothyroxine sodium

Used to replace thyroid hormone in clients with hypothyroidism.
Medication must be taken for entire lifetime, at same time each day and on an empty stomach.
Toxicity symptoms include: tachycardia, chest pain, palpations, and excessive sweathing.

Metoclopramide

Antiemetic medication used to tx nausea, vomiting, and gastroparesis by increasing gastrointestinal motility and promoting stomach empyting.
Extended used/high doses may lead to tardive dyskinesia.
Uncontrollable motions (sucking/smacking lip motion)

Enoxaparin, dalteparin

Anticoagulants, used for prevention and tx of DVT & pulmonary embolism.

Levodopa, Carbidopa

Levodopa, converted to dopamine in the brain.
Carbidopa helps prevent breakdown of levodopa before it can reach the brain and take effect.
Used for tx of bradykinesia. Medication should never be stopped suddenly.

Thrombolytic agents

Alteplase, tenecteplase, reteplase
Place pt at risk for bleeding.
Contraindicated in clients with active bleeding, recent trauma, aneurysm, arteriovenous malformation, history of hemorrhagic stroke, and uncontrolled hypertension.

Sumatriptan

Work by constricting cranial blood vessels and clients should be instructed to take a dose at the first sign of a migraine to help prevent and relieve symptoms.

Adalimumub

Tumor necrosis factor inhibitor
Major adverse effect include immunosuppression and infection.
Elevated WBC should be reported to HCP.

Pyelonephritis

Nausea & Vomiting, fever & chills, flank pain, costovertebral tenderness

Cystitis

Inflamed bladder Buring on urination, frequency, urgency, suprapubic discomfort, hematuria.

Colostomy irrigation

Fill irrigation container with 500-1000ml lukewarm water, flush irrigation tubing and reclamp.
Hang container on the a hook or IV pole
Instruct pt to sit on toilet, place irrigation tubing 18-24 inches above the stoma.
Lubricate cone-tipped irrigator, insert cone and attached catheter gently into the stoma, and hold in place.
Slowly open the roller clamp, allowing irrigation solution to flow for 5-10 minutes.
Clamp the tubing if cramping occurs, until it subsides
Once the desired amount of solution is instilled, the cone is removed and feces is allowed to drain through the sleeve into the toilet.

Cardiac catheterization

Involves injection of Iodine contrast to examine obstructed coronary arteries.
Complications include: Allergic reaction, contrast nephropathy, lactic acidosis.
Client with renal impairment should not receive IV contrast unless absolutely necessary.
Metformin w/ IV contrast increases risk for lactic acidosis

Brachytherapy

Internal radiation, involves direct application of a radioactive implant.
Time spent near the radiation source is restricted, 30 min per shift. Cluster care, no pregnant individuals or under 18.
Keep a maximum distance away from pt, 6 feet. Private room, private bath, pt to remain on bedrest.
Shielding w/ lead to diminish radiation exposure.

Fat embolism

Respiratory problems, neurologic changes, petechial rash and fever > 101.4.

Pressure Alarms

High: Kinked tubing, obstruction, biting, increase resistance, decreased lung compliance.
Low: Disconnected tubing, leaking cuff, loss of airway.

Contact precautions

MRSA, VRE, CDiff, Scabies
Hand hygiene (soap & water for Cdiff), nonsterile gloves, gown, private room preferred.

Von Willebrand disease

Bleeding disorder caused by deficiency of von willebrand factor.
Pt should wear medical bracelet

Hemophilia

Bleeding disorder caused by deficiency in coagulation proteins.
RN should avoid procedures that can cause bleeding.

Polycythemia vera

Chronic disorder of bone marrow in which excessive amounts of RBC, WBC, and Plts are produced.
Pt are at risk of developing blood clots d/t increased volume, viscosity, and stasis of their blood.
Monitor for s/s of thrombus: redness, tenderness, and swelling in the legs or symptoms of stroke.
Teach pt preventative measures: used support stockings, elevate the legs when sitting and hydrate properly.

Pneumothorax

Collapsed lung
Hyper-resonance to percussion, diminished breath sounds
Decreased tactile vocal fremitus
Hypotension: decreased venous return

Metabolic Acidosis

Common causes:
GI bicarbonate losses (diarrhea)
Ketoacidosis (diabetes, alcoholism, starvation)
Lactic acidosis (sepsis, hypoperfusion)
Renal failure (hemodialysis)
Salicylate toxicity

Serotonine Syndrome

Diaphoresis, Agitation, Tachycardia, Mydriasis
Clonus, tremors, hyperreflexia
Autonomic instability & hypertension
Diarrhea, hyperactive bowl sounds

Magnesium Sulfate Toxicity

Decreased: blood pressure, urine output, respiratory rate, deep tendon reflexes
Calcium gluconate = reversal agent

Hypoglycemia

Tremors, hunger, irritability, nervousness

Hypercalcemia

increase urine volume, excessive thirst, nausea, vomiting
constipation, decreased muscle tone, flank pain
